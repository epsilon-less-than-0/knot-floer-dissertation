\chapter[Splitting Sequences for the Stratum $(2;1^6;3^2)$]{Splitting Sequences for the Stratum $(2;1^6;3^2)$}
\label{app:splitting_big_stratum}

This appendix collects the exhaustive tight-splitting diagrams for the base train tracks of the stratum $(2; 1^6; 3^2)$ that arise in the proof of Lemma \ref{lem:two_triangle_reduction} of Chapter \ref{chap:stratatraintrackanalysis} (Figures \ref{fig:19_splitting}--\ref{fig:1_splitting}). Together with the mirror-pair identification carried out there, these diagrams establish the second stage of the reduction of the geometric state space, from the jointless \texttt{autofolder} output for this stratum to the six surviving standard train tracks $(0), (1), (8), (12), (13), (17)$ recorded in Figure \ref{fig:final_six_tracks}.

\paragraph{What the diagrams show.} Each figure depicts a single train track $\tau$ (numbered by its label in the \texttt{autofolder} output for the stratum) together with the tree of tight splittings emanating from $\tau$. The root of each tree is $\tau$ itself; the leaves are the tracks reached after a finite sequence of tight splittings. By Proposition \ref{prop:carrying_preserved_under_split}, a tight splitting preserves the carrying property: any pseudo-Anosov map carried by $\tau$ is carried, up to conjugation by a braid, by each track in the splitting tree; by Proposition \ref{prop:transition_matrix_split} the associated dynamics (transition matrix, and hence dilatation) are preserved as well. Consequently, classifying the pseudo-Anosov maps carried by $\tau$ reduces to classifying those carried by the leaves of its tree.

\paragraph{How to read the diagrams.} Each diagram is a splitting diagram in the sense of Section \ref{sec:switch-rigidity}: a finite directed graph of tight $l$- and $r$-splits rooted at $\tau$. A diagram may contain splitting loops, but by the analysis of that section every such loop is excluded. A loop returning to a jointless track carries the periodic loop braid $\delta^{-1} = \sigma_2^{-1}\sigma_1^{-1}$ (up to conjugacy and full twists) and is ruled out by Lemma \ref{lem:periodic_loop}; a loop retaining a joint is ruled out by Reinoso's Theorem~C (Lemma \ref{lem:jointed_loop}). The leaves recorded in each caption are therefore the tracks reached upon exiting every loop. Each such leaf is either one of the six surviving tracks $(0), (1), (8), (12), (13), (17)$, the root of another diagram in this appendix, or the vertical mirror image of such a track. Tracing the diagrams transitively and identifying mirror pairs (as in the proof of Lemma \ref{lem:two_triangle_reduction}), every base track of the stratum reduces to one of the six surviving tracks.

\paragraph{Consequences for the enumeration.} Combined with the mirror-pair identification of Lemma \ref{lem:two_triangle_reduction}, the splitting diagrams collected here establish that, up to conjugation and mirroring, every pseudo-Anosov mapping class in the stratum $(2; 1^6; 3^2)$ is carried by one of the six surviving tracks of Figure \ref{fig:final_six_tracks}. This is the geometric reduction underlying Proposition \ref{prop:two_triangle_braids} and the enumeration of candidate braid families that follows.
