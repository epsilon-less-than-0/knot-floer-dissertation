\chapter{Proof of the Main Theorem}\label{chap:proof_of_main_theorem}

Having developed in the preceding chapters all of the topological, dynamical, and homological machinery required to constrain the monodromy of a hypothetical knot sharing the knot Floer homology of $T_{2,3}\# T_{2,3}$, we now assemble these results into a proof of the primary result of this dissertation, Theorem \ref{thm:main_detection}.

The argument proceeds along Thurston's classification of knots (Theorem \ref{theorem:thurston classification of knots}): the knot $K$ is a torus knot, a hyperbolic knot, or a satellite knot, and these three possibilities are mutually exclusive at the level of the knot exterior $E(K)$. The case division is by knot type throughout; the Nielsen-Thurston type of the monodromy is used only as a tool inside the hyperbolic and satellite cases, and at no point do we appeal to exclusivity among the periodic, reducible, and pseudo-Anosov surface classes. We eliminate the torus and hyperbolic cases entirely and use the satellite analysis of Chapter \ref{chap:satellite} to identify the remaining case with the connect-sum of two trefoils, with the residual chirality question resolved inline by inspection of the Maslov-grading support of $\widehat{HFK}$.

\section{Proof of Theorem \ref{thm:main_detection}}
\label{sec:proof_main_detection}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Theorem \ref{thm:main_detection}]
Let $K \subset S^3$ be a knot such that
\[
\widehat{HFK}(K; \mathbb{F}) \cong \widehat{HFK}(T_{2,3} \# T_{2,3}; \mathbb{F})
\]
as bigraded vector spaces over $\mathbb{F} = \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$. We must show that $K$ is isotopic to $T_{2,3} \# T_{2,3}$.

\paragraph{Initial Reductions.}
The Euler characteristic of $\widehat{HFK}$ recovers the Alexander polynomial:
\[
\Delta_K(t) = \sum_{M,A} (-1)^M \dim_{\mathbb{F}} \widehat{HFK}_M(K, A) \cdot t^A = (t^2 - t + 1)^2.
\]
By Theorem \ref{theorem:ni-fibered} and the genus-detection result of Ozsv\'{a}th--Szab\'{o} (Equation \eqref{eq:ozvath-szabo genus top grading}), the rank profile of $\widehat{HFK}(K)$ in its top Alexander grading forces $K$ to be a fibered knot of genus exactly $g = 2$. Let
\[
h \colon \Sigma_2^1 \to \Sigma_2^1
\]
denote its monodromy, defined up to isotopy fixing $\partial \Sigma_2^1$ pointwise.

By Thurston's classification of knots (Theorem \ref{theorem:thurston classification of knots}), $K$ is exactly one of three types: a torus knot, a hyperbolic knot, or a satellite knot. This exclusive partition is read off the geometry of the exterior $E(K)$ --- atoroidal Seifert-fibered, atoroidal non-Seifert-fibered, or toroidal --- and is the sole source of exclusivity in what follows. Within each case we will, where convenient, invoke the Nielsen-Thurston type of $h$ as a tool, via the correspondence between the geometry of the mapping torus $M_h = E(K)$ and the dynamics of $h$; but we never use the surface classification (Theorem \ref{theorem:nielsen-thruston classification}) to \emph{partition} the cases, and in particular we never assume the periodic, reducible, and pseudo-Anosov types to be mutually exclusive. We treat each knot type in turn.

\paragraph{Case 1: $K$ is a torus knot.}
Suppose $K$ is a torus knot, $K = T_{p,q}$ for some coprime integers $p, q$ with $|p|, |q| \ge 2$. Every torus knot in $S^3$ is an L-space knot (or the mirror of one), and a defining property of L-space knots is that their knot Floer homology has rank at most $1$ in every Alexander grading:
\[
\dim_{\mathbb{F}} \widehat{HFK}(T_{p,q}, a) \le 1 \quad \text{for all } a \in \mathbb{Z}.
\]
However, an explicit calculation of $\widehat{HFK}(T_{2,3}\# T_{2,3})$ shows that the next-to-top Alexander grading has rank exactly $2$:
\[
\dim_{\mathbb{F}} \widehat{HFK}(T_{2,3}\# T_{2,3}, 1) = 2.
\]
Since the bigraded isomorphism $\widehat{HFK}(K) \cong \widehat{HFK}(T_{2,3}\# T_{2,3})$ would force $\dim_{\mathbb{F}} \widehat{HFK}(K, 1) = 2$, $K$ cannot be a torus knot.

\paragraph{Case 2: $K$ is a hyperbolic knot.}
Suppose $K$ is hyperbolic, so that $S^3 \setminus K$ admits a complete hyperbolic metric of finite volume. Since $K$ is fibered, Thurston's fibered hyperbolization (Theorem \ref{theorem:Thurston hyperbolic iff pA}) then forces the monodromy $h$ to be pseudo-Anosov; let $\phi_h$ denote its unique pseudo-Anosov representative.

By Lemma \ref{lemma:original monodromy has one fixed point}, $\phi_h$ has exactly one fixed point in the interior of $\Sigma_2^1$. By the capping-off construction (Proposition \ref{prop:capping off}) and the Birman-Hilden correspondence (Section \ref{section:Birman-Hilden}), this monodromy descends to a 6-braid mapping class $\beta \in \mathrm{Mod}(D_6)$ on the marked disk, whose canonical lift to the genus-two surface with two boundary components $\Sigma_2^2$ is fixed-point-free in its interior. Concretely, the data of $K$ thus assembles into the Birman-Hilden correspondence tuple
\[
(h, \phi_h, \hat{h}, \hat{\phi}_h, b, \phi_b, \beta, \phi_\beta, \Phi, \phi_\Phi).
\]
The Euler-Poincar\'{e} analysis carried out in Section \ref{sec:euler_poincare} of the Preliminaries restricts the singularity stratum of $\phi_\beta$ on the disk to one of five possibilities:
\[
(2; 1^4, 2^2; \emptyset),\quad (4; 1^6; \emptyset),\quad (2; 1^6; 4),\quad (3; 1^6; 3),\quad (2; 1^6; 3^2).
\]
We have eliminated all five strata across Chapters \ref{chap:stratoriented} and \ref{chap:stratatraintrackanalysis}:

\begin{itemize}
    \item By Theorem \ref{thm:orientable_strata_elimination}, the orientable strata $(2; 1^4, 2^2; \emptyset)$, $(4; 1^6; \emptyset)$, and $(2; 1^6; 4)$ are eliminated by an Alexander polynomial obstruction: any such stratum forces the lifted invariant foliations on $\Sigma_2^2$ to be orientable, but $\Delta_K(t) = (t^2 - t + 1)^2$ has no real roots, contradicting Thurston's identification of the dilatation with a real eigenvalue of the homological action.
    \item By Theorem \ref{thm:candelabra_stratum_classification}, the non-orientable stratum $(3; 1^6; 3)$ is eliminated: the \texttt{autofolder} algorithm together with tight splitting reduces the candidate train tracks to the Candelabra, and exhaustive enumeration via the Trace Lemma and the Absorption Obstruction produces 38 candidate braid families. Homological filtering---requiring both $|\det(I - A_{\text{red}})| = 1$ (so that the underlying 3-manifold of the open book is an integer homology sphere) and $\det(tI - A_{\text{red}}) = (t^2-t+1)^2$ (matching $\Delta_K$)---eliminates all 38 (Appendix \ref{app:granny_verdict_tables}).
    \item By Theorem \ref{thm:two_triangle_stratum_classification}, the non-orientable stratum $(2; 1^6; 3^2)$ is eliminated by the parallel analysis: tight splitting reduces the 20 candidate jointless tracks to six (one of which, Track 12, admits no surviving train track maps), exhaustive enumeration on the remaining five tracks produces 49 candidate braid families in total (13 for Track 0, 17 for Track 1, 9 for Track 8, 4 for Track 13, and 6 for Track 17), and the same homological filters eliminate them all (Appendix \ref{app:granny_verdict_tables}).
\end{itemize}

These results combine to establish the obstruction theorem promised in the introduction:

\begin{theorem}[\ref{thm:homological_elimination}, restated]
None of the fixed-point-free pseudo-Anosov mapping classes generated by the standard train tracks in the admissible strata $(2; 1^4, 2^2; \emptyset)$, $(4; 1^6; \emptyset)$, $(2; 1^6; 4)$, $(3; 1^6; 3)$, and $(2; 1^6; 3^2)$ on the 6-marked disk induce both an Alexander polynomial equal to $\Delta(t) = (t^2-t+1)^2$ and a 3-manifold (the underlying space of the associated open book) that is an integer homology sphere.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The orientable strata are dispatched by Theorem \ref{thm:orientable_strata_elimination}. The non-orientable strata $(3; 1^6; 3)$ and $(2; 1^6; 3^2)$ are dispatched by Theorems \ref{thm:candelabra_stratum_classification} and \ref{thm:two_triangle_stratum_classification}, respectively. By the Euler-Poincar\'{e} classification of Section \ref{sec:euler_poincare}, these are exhaustive.
\end{proof}

Consequently, no pseudo-Anosov mapping class $\phi_\beta$ on $D_6$ with the required fixed-point structure can correspond to the monodromy of $K$. This contradicts the assumption that $h$ is pseudo-Anosov, so $K$ is not hyperbolic.

\paragraph{Case 3: $K$ is a satellite knot.}
Having eliminated the torus and hyperbolic cases, Thurston's classification of knots (Theorem \ref{theorem:thurston classification of knots}), exclusive at the level of the knot exterior, leaves only the possibility that $K$ is a satellite knot. We stress that this conclusion uses the knot trichotomy directly, not any exclusivity among the surface Nielsen-Thurston types; we do \emph{not} argue that $h$ is reducible by eliminating the periodic and pseudo-Anosov classes.

Being a satellite, $K$ is not a torus knot (Theorem \ref{theorem:thurston classification of knots}), so its monodromy $h$ is not periodic and the mapping torus $M_h = E(K)$ is not Seifert fibered. The essential torus furnished by the satellite structure is therefore isotopic to a vertical torus --- the suspension of an $h$-invariant essential multicurve --- so the canonical reducing system $\Gamma$ of $h$ is nonempty (the fibered correspondence: for aperiodic $h$, an essential torus in $M_h$ is equivalent to a nonempty canonical reducing system; see Theorem \ref{theorem:thurston_reducing_system} and Remark \ref{rem:global_NT_type}). With $\Gamma \neq \emptyset$ in hand, Theorem \ref{theorem:satellite}, established in Chapter \ref{chap:satellite}, shows that the only satellite configuration consistent with $\Delta_K(t) = (t^2-t+1)^2$ and the genus-two fiberedness constraint is the connect-sum of two trefoils.

\begin{remark}
A parallel detection question for the $(2,1)$-cable $C_{2,1}(T_{2,3})$ --- which is itself a fibered genus-two satellite knot --- is taken up separately in Chapter \ref{chap:cable_detection}, where we establish a partial detection theorem for that knot. Note that the $(2,1)$-cable is not a candidate for $K$ in the present proof, because Lemma \ref{lem:satellite_trefoils_windingnumber_1} excludes it (its winding number is $2$, not $1$, and its pattern $P(U) = T_{2,1}$ is the unknot rather than a trefoil).
\end{remark}

The remaining configuration is the connect-sum of two trefoils. The detailed argument of Chapter \ref{chap:satellite} proceeds as follows. Schubert's formula (Theorem \ref{thm:schubert}) and the explicit factorization of $\Delta_K(t) = (t^2-t+1)^2 = t^2 - 2t + 3 - 2t^{-1} + t^{-2}$ force the algebraic winding number to equal $1$ and identify both the pattern $P(U)$ and the companion $C$ as trefoils (Lemma \ref{lem:satellite_trefoils_windingnumber_1}). The classification of canonical reducing systems (Lemma \ref{lem:satellite_CaseACaseB}) presents two non-trivial geometric configurations: Case A (the outer surface $\Sigma_0$ is a pair of pants) and Case B (the outer surface is genus one). The pair-of-pants case admits no pseudo-Anosov outer map, forcing the outer map to be periodic; combined with Baldwin-Sivek's Proposition \ref{prop:knottracesprop} and the genus-two constraint, this yields the connect-sum of two genus-one knots, of which only the trefoil-trefoil connect sum (up to chirality of the two factors) has the correct Alexander polynomial. The genus-one case is eliminated by a fractional Dehn twist coefficient analysis: the Hedden-Mark bound $|c(K)| \le 1$ forces $|c(K)| = 1$, but the contact-invariant argument via Honda-Kazez-Mati\'{c} rules out integer twist coefficients.

Therefore, $K$ is isotopic to a connect-sum of two trefoils, taken with some choice of chirality. The three candidates are $T_{2,3} \# T_{2,3}$ (the granny knot, homochiral), $T_{2,3} \# \overline{T}_{2,3}$ (the square knot, heterochiral), and $\overline{T}_{2,3} \# \overline{T}_{2,3}$ (the mirror granny), shown in Figure \ref{fig:three_candidates}.

All three share the Alexander polynomial $(t^2-t+1)^2$ and the total $\widehat{HFK}$-rank of $9$, so the unbigraded $\widehat{HFK}$ does not distinguish them. However, the K\"{u}nneth formula combined with the mirror duality $\widehat{HFK}_M(\overline{K}, A) \cong \widehat{HFK}_{-M}(K, -A)$ \cite{OS04Knot} shows that the top Alexander grading $A = 2$ is supported in Maslov grading $0$ for $T_{2,3}\# T_{2,3}$, in Maslov grading $2$ for $T_{2,3}\#\overline{T}_{2,3}$, and in Maslov grading $4$ for $\overline{T}_{2,3}\#\overline{T}_{2,3}$; equivalently, the Ozsv\'{a}th-Szab\'{o} concordance invariant $\tau$ takes the values $2$, $0$, and $-2$ respectively. Since the bigraded isomorphism $\widehat{HFK}(K) \cong \widehat{HFK}(T_{2,3}\# T_{2,3})$ matches Maslov gradings, only the homochiral case is compatible with the hypothesis. Therefore
\[
K \cong T_{2,3} \# T_{2,3}.
\]
This concludes the proof.
\end{proof}

\section{Summary}

We have established the full strength of Theorem \ref{thm:main_detection}: any knot $K \subset S^3$ whose knot Floer homology agrees with that of the granny knot $T_{2,3}\# T_{2,3}$ as a bigraded vector space over $\mathbb{F} = \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$ is isotopic to $T_{2,3}\# T_{2,3}$ itself. The proof rests on the following structural pillars:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item The genus-and-fiberedness detection results of Ozsv\'{a}th-Szab\'{o}, Ni, and Juh\'{a}sz, which transfer the question from three-dimensional topology to surface dynamics.
    \item The Birman-Hilden correspondence, which descends the genus-two monodromy to a 6-braid on the disk.
    \item The autofolder algorithm and tight splitting (Chapters \ref{chap:folding_automata}, \ref{chap:stratatraintrackanalysis}), which generate and prune the finite list of candidate train tracks for each non-orientable stratum.
    \item The Trace Lemma and Absorption Obstruction (Chapters \ref{chap:train tracks}, \ref{chap:stratatraintrackanalysis}), which encode fixed-point and injectivity constraints on the train track maps.
    \item The orientability obstruction of Chapter \ref{chap:stratoriented}, which dispatches the orientable strata via the absence of real Alexander roots.
    \item The homological filtering pipeline (Section \ref{sec:homological_filtering}), which eliminates the candidate braid families via $|\det(I - A_{\text{red}})| = 1$ and $\det(tI - A_{\text{red}}) = (t^2-t+1)^2$, with the verdicts catalogued in Appendix \ref{app:granny_verdict_tables}.
    \item The satellite analysis of Chapter \ref{chap:satellite}, which combines Schubert's formula, the Baldwin-Sivek classification of canonical reducing systems, and the Hedden-Mark / Honda-Kazez-Mati\'{c} contact-invariant arguments to identify the connect-sum of two trefoils as the unique surviving satellite candidate.
\end{enumerate}

A parallel partial detection theorem for the $(2,1)$-cable of the trefoil, $C_{2,1}(T_{2,3})$, is established in Chapter \ref{chap:cable_detection}, where the same machinery---autofolder, Trace Lemma, Absorption Obstruction, and homological filtering---is deployed against the cable target Alexander polynomial $t^4 - t^2 + 1$.
